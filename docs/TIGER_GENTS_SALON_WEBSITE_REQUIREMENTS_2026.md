## 1. Executive Summary

Build a world-class digital presence for **Tiger Gents Salon** that feels more like a premium hospitality, fashion, automotive, or private-members-club website than a conventional barber template.

The website must communicate:

1. **High-end service**
2. **High-quality grooming products**
3. **Timely, dependable appointments**
4. **Consistent service standards**
5. **Personal care**
6. **Attention to detail**
7. **Expert professionals**
8. **Comfort, confidence, and discretion**
9. **Modern grooming in the heart of Business Bay**
10. **Simple and immediate booking through Fresha**

The website must look luxurious without becoming theatrical, difficult to use, slow, overly dark, or visually cluttered. Luxury should come from **restraint, craft, proportion, photography, typography, rhythm, consistency, and detail**, not from adding excessive gold, animation, glow, gradients, or decorative effects.

The experience should balance:

- **Quiet luxury:** restraint, space, fine typography, confidence
- **Modern 2026 design:** editorial composition, subtle motion, material depth, responsive typography, and tailored interaction
- **Commercial performance:** prominent booking actions, service clarity, social proof, location information, and frictionless navigation
- **Operational credibility:** clear opening hours, realistic service expectations, actual team members, real reviews, and accurate information
- **Technical excellence:** accessibility, fast loading, stable layout, local SEO, and analytics

## 9. Luxury Design Principles

Luxury is not a color. It is the cumulative result of controlled decisions.

### 9.1 Restraint

- Use fewer elements, executed better.
- Do not fill every area.
- Do not apply gold to all headings and borders.
- Limit decorative devices to one coherent system.
- Give key content room to breathe.

### 9.2 Hierarchy

Every screen must have:

1. One dominant message
2. One primary action
3. Supporting content
4. Quiet tertiary details

Do not let navigation, decorative lines, statistics, social icons, and multiple buttons compete with the hero.

### 9.3 Materiality

Create depth through:

- Real photography
- Fine grain
- Paper, leather, brushed metal, glass, stone, and warm wood references
- Soft shadow and restrained blur
- Tonal contrast
- Subtle highlights

Do not simulate luxury through heavy bevels, fake chrome, excessive gloss, or yellow gradients.

### 9.4 Craft

- Perfect alignment
- Consistent radii
- Purposeful spacing
- Optical logo sizing
- Fine typography
- Quality iconography
- Smooth transitions
- Clean states and error handling
- No default browser-looking controls

### 9.5 Confidence

- Large editorial typography
- Strong but simple statements
- No crowded sales banners
- No desperate discounts above the fold
- No excessive trust-badge clutter
- No autoplay sound

### 9.6 Human warmth

A premium site must not feel cold. Include:

- Real faces
- Real hands at work
- Consultation moments
- Hospitality details
- Product preparation
- Client comfort
- Direct, human copy

## 10. 2026 Design Trend Strategy

Apply 2026 trends selectively. The website must feel current for several years, not trendy for six months.

### 10.1 Trends to incorporate

#### A. Editorial asymmetry

- Offset text and imagery on large screens
- Use intentional negative space
- Break the grid only for expressive sections
- Return to a strict grid for service comparison and booking information

#### B. Responsive variable typography

- Use fluid type with `clamp()`
- Allow display headings to scale smoothly
- Consider a variable font for reduced file weight and fine control
- Use subtle width or weight animation only in hero moments

#### C. Cinematic scrollytelling

- One or two controlled scroll sequences
- Reveal craftsmanship details as users scroll
- Use pinned content sparingly
- Never prevent normal scrolling
- Disable or simplify on low-powered devices and reduced-motion settings

#### D. Layered material depth

- Fine grain
- Semi-transparent surfaces only where legibility remains strong
- Brushed brass lines
- Warm photographic shadows
- Soft focus transitions
- Subtle foreground/background separation

#### E. Kinetic typography

- Gentle letter-spacing or mask reveal in hero
- No bouncing text
- No continuous animation
- Motion must stop after completing
- Avoid animated body copy

#### F. Micro-interactions

- Tactile button response
- Smooth underline movement
- Image desaturation-to-color hover where appropriate
- Card depth or crop shift
- Clear booking-link feedback
- Visible keyboard-focus states

#### G. Tailored personalization

Optional “Find your service” assistant:

- Ask 3–5 simple questions
- Recommend service categories
- Never pretend to diagnose hair or skin
- Never replace professional consultation
- Link to matching Fresha service category
- Do not force chatbot interaction

#### H. Performance-conscious design

Modern luxury should load quickly. Use:

- Responsive images
- AVIF/WebP
- Efficient video
- Selective animation
- Minimal JavaScript
- Font subsetting
- Server rendering where appropriate

#### I. Ethical design

- No dark patterns
- No fake countdowns
- No preselected marketing consent
- No hidden prices
- No misleading reviews
- No false scarcity

### 10.2 Trends to avoid or heavily limit

- Full-site “liquid glass”
- Excessive glassmorphism
- Neumorphic controls
- Generic bento grids everywhere
- Large 3D tiger animation
- Custom cursor that reduces usability
- Horizontal scroll for core content
- Intro splash screen
- Long preloader
- Autoplay audio
- Motion on every section
- Overlapping text that harms readability
- Brutalist or intentionally broken navigation
- AI chatbot opening automatically
- Excessive parallax
- Overuse of gold glow

- 70–80% obsidian/charcoal

- Do not use pure black for every surface.
- Do not use pure white for long body text.

- Gold text must not be used for small low-contrast copy.
- Avoid red and blue barber-pole colors in the core premium identity.
- A red accent may appear only in a heritage submark if the owner explicitly approves it.

### 11.3 Texture

Allowed:

- Very subtle monochrome grain at 1–3% opacity
- Fine brushed-metal texture in close detail
- Dark leather and stone photography
- Warm shadow falloff
- Subtle tiger-stripe watermark

Avoid:

- Fake marble backgrounds
- Repeating carbon fiber
- Heavy noise
- Sparkles
- Lens flare
- Gold foil on all components

## 12. Typography

### 12.1 Recommended typography pairing

#### Premium licensed option

- Display/editorial: **Canela**, **Noe Display**, or a comparable licensed high-contrast serif
- UI/body: **Suisse Int’l**, **Neue Haas Grotesk**, or comparable licensed neutral sans
- Wordmark: custom-drawn lettering, not a standard font

#### High-quality open-source option

- Display/editorial: **Bodoni Moda Variable**
- UI/body: **Manrope Variable**
- Numbers/technical details: Manrope with tabular numerals
- Arabic future support: **Noto Sans Arabic** or **IBM Plex Sans Arabic**

### 12.2 Typography roles

| Role | Desktop | Mobile | Guidance |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Hero display | 72–116 px fluid | 46–64 px fluid | 0.9–1.0 line-height |
| H1 | 64–88 px | 42–56 px | One H1 per page |
| H2 | 44–64 px | 34–44 px | Editorial hierarchy |
| H3 | 28–38 px | 25–32 px | Service/team headings |
| Body large | 19–22 px | 18–20 px | Hero support and intros |
| Body | 16–18 px | 16–18 px | Minimum comfortable size |
| Small | 13–14 px | 13–14 px | Metadata only |
| Eyebrow | 11–13 px | 11–12 px | Uppercase with tracking |

### 12.3 Typography rules

- Maximum body line length: 60–72 characters
- Large editorial copy: 8–12 words per line where possible
- Body line-height: 1.55–1.75
- Do not use condensed type for long text
- Limit uppercase to labels, navigation, and small accents
- Avoid letter spacing above `0.16em`
- Avoid ultra-thin weights on dark backgrounds
- Use optical kerning
- Use `font-display: swap` or `optional`
- Preload only the essential font file
- Subset fonts where legally and technically permitted

## 13. Logo Redesign Requirements

### 13.1 Objective

Evolve the supplied logo into a premium, contemporary brand mark while preserving recognisable Tiger DNA.

### 13.2 Problems in the supplied logo to solve

- Too many competing elements
- Mixed visual styles
- Clip-art-like scissors
- Tiger, barber pole, stripes, letterforms, gradient, and subheading all competing
- Weak performance at small sizes
- Inconsistent stroke weights
- Limited spacing discipline
- Black/red/white pole feels more conventional than premium
- Wordmark proportions are not optimised for a website header
- Fine details will disappear in favicon and mobile contexts

### 13.3 Recommended logo concept

#### Primary direction

A custom **TIGER** wordmark with:

- Refined tiger head integrated above or within the `I`
- The `I` subtly referencing a barber pole through negative space, not red/blue stripes
- Controlled tiger-stripe cuts inside one or two letters only
- Strong geometric structure
- Premium custom letterforms
- Balanced width suitable for desktop and mobile headers
- Supporting line: `GENTS SALON` or `BARBERSHOP`, based on final legal/brand naming
- No scissors in the primary horizontal mark

#### Secondary emblem

- Simplified tiger head
- Optional subtle barber-pole stem
- Circular or shield composition
- Suitable for favicon, social profile, towel embroidery, product labels, and app icon

#### Heritage seal

Scissors may appear in a secondary heritage seal, not in the core navigation logo.

### 13.4 Logo style

- Flat vector master
- Monoline or controlled mixed-line tiger illustration
- Strong silhouette
- No photorealism
- No 3D bevel
- No heavy gradient in the master
- No drop shadow in the master
- Optional metallic brass rendering for campaign imagery only
- Must work in one color

### 13.5 Logo colors

Primary dark-background version:

- Tiger/wordmark: warm ivory
- Accent eye or minimal line: burnished brass
- Supporting text: muted stone or ivory

Primary light-background version:

- Tiger/wordmark: obsidian
- Accent: burnished brass

Monochrome versions:

- 100% black
- 100% white
- Single brass color

### 13.6 Logo dimensions and clear space

#### SVG artboards

- Primary horizontal: `720 × 240`
- Compact horizontal: `600 × 200`
- Stacked: `480 × 560`
- Emblem: `512 × 512`

These dimensions define artboard proportions; SVG remains resolution independent.

#### Display sizes

Desktop header:

- Recommended rendered width: `168–196 px`
- Recommended rendered height: `48–58 px`

Mobile header:

- Recommended rendered width: `128–150 px`
- Recommended rendered height: `38–46 px`

- Recommended rendered width: `180–220 px`

#### Clear space

- Minimum clear space around logo: height of the `T` crossbar or 20% of emblem width
- No text, border, or image edge may enter clear space

## 14. Layout and Spacing System

### 14.1 Grid

Desktop:

- 12-column grid
- Maximum content width: `1440 px`
- Preferred standard content width: `1280 px`
- Reading content width: `720–820 px`
- Gutter: `24–32 px`
- Outer margins: `64–96 px`

Tablet:

- 8-column grid
- Outer margins: `32–48 px`
- Gutter: `20–24 px`

Mobile:

- 4-column grid
- Outer margins: `20 px`
- Compact devices: `16 px`
- Gutter: `12–16 px`

### 14.2 Base spacing scale

Use an 8 px system with 4 px exceptions.

```text
4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 40, 48, 64, 80, 96, 120, 144, 160, 192
```

### 14.3 Section spacing

Desktop:

- Hero internal padding: 120–160 px top and bottom
- Major sections: 144–192 px vertical
- Standard sections: 112–144 px vertical
- Compact sections: 72–96 px vertical

Tablet:

- Major sections: 96–128 px
- Standard sections: 80–96 px

Mobile:

- Major sections: 72–96 px
- Standard sections: 56–72 px
- Compact sections: 40–56 px

### 14.4 Card spacing

- Card padding desktop: 28–36 px
- Card padding mobile: 20–24 px
- Card gap desktop: 24–32 px
- Card gap mobile: 16–20 px
- Metadata gap: 8–12 px
- Heading-to-copy gap: 16–20 px
- Copy-to-action gap: 24–32 px

### 14.5 Spacing psychology

- High-value information receives more surrounding space.
- Price and duration must not be visually buried.
- Do not put borders around every item.
- Use alignment and spacing before using boxes.
- Maintain consistent vertical rhythm across pages.
- Dense service lists may be compact, but hero, standards, and story sections must remain spacious.

## 15. Responsive Breakpoints

Use content-driven breakpoints, with these testing anchors:

```text
360 px   — minimum supported small mobile
390 px   — common mobile
430 px   — large mobile
768 px   — portrait tablet
1024 px  — tablet / small laptop
1280 px  — standard desktop
1440 px  — large desktop
1728 px  — wide desktop
```

### 15.1 Responsive principles

- Mobile is not a shrunken desktop.
- Reorder content based on mobile intent.
- Booking must remain visible.
- Core navigation must not be hidden behind ambiguous gestures.
- Decorative motion should reduce automatically.
- Avoid text over complex imagery on small screens.
- Team and service cards must be fully readable without hover.
- No horizontal page overflow.
- Do not require users to swipe horizontal carousels to discover all essential services.
- Use carousels only for supplemental media.

## 23. Interaction Design

### 23.1 Button hierarchy

Primary:

- Solid brass background
- Obsidian text
- Minimum 48 px height
- Strong contrast

Secondary:

- Transparent or charcoal surface
- Ivory text
- Fine brass or stone border

Tertiary:

- Text link with directional arrow
- Clear hover/focus underline

### 23.2 Button copy

Use action-oriented labels:

- Book appointment
- Check availability
- View services
- Book with Joe
- Get directions
- Meet the team

Avoid vague labels:

- Discover
- Explore more
- Click here
- Submit
- Learn more, when a more specific label is available

### 23.3 Motion timings

- Button response: 120–180 ms
- Hover transition: 180–260 ms
- Menu transition: 280–420 ms
- Section reveal: 500–800 ms
- Hero reveal: 700–1100 ms
- Use natural easing, not elastic bounce

Recommended easing:

```css
--ease-premium: cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1);
--ease-standard: cubic-bezier(0.2, 0, 0, 1);
```

### 23.4 Reduced motion

Respect:

```css
@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)
```

Under reduced motion:

- Remove parallax
- Remove pinned scroll
- Replace video with poster where possible
- Disable letter-by-letter animation
- Keep simple fades under 150 ms or remove them
- Preserve all information and functionality

### 23.5 Hover and focus

Hover must never be the only way to access information.

Focus:

- Visible 2–3 px outline
- Brass or ivory with sufficient contrast
- Offset from component
- Never hidden by sticky header or bottom bar

- Semantic HTML
- Logical heading order
- One H1 per page
- Keyboard-accessible navigation
- Visible focus

- Large text contrast at least 3:1
- UI-component contrast at least 3:1
- Controls at least 44 × 44 px preferred
- 24 × 24 px absolute minimum only where compliant spacing exists
- Alt text for meaningful images
- Empty alt for decorative images
- Captions for meaningful video
- No information conveyed only by color
- Forms with real labels
- Error messages associated with controls
- Zoom to 200% without loss of functionality
- Reflow at 320 CSS px
- Reduced-motion support
- No keyboard traps
- Dialog focus management
- Menu focus management
- Accessible lightbox
- Accessible carousel controls
- Language attribute on HTML
- RTL-ready architecture for future Arabic

- Do not use low-opacity grey for essential copy.

## 44. Final Instruction to the Coding Agent

Build the site as a premium product, not as a themed template.

Prioritise in this order:

1. Accuracy
2. Booking usability
3. Mobile experience
4. Accessibility
5. Performance
6. Visual hierarchy
7. Real photography
8. Typography and spacing
9. Motion
10. Decorative effects

Every design choice should answer at least one of these questions:

- Does it communicate quality?
- Does it communicate personal care?
- Does it demonstrate attention to detail?
- Does it make booking easier?
- Does it respect the visitor’s time?
- Does it improve trust?
- Does it make the service easier to understand?

If it answers none of them, remove it.

The finished website should feel calm, precise, confident, modern, and unmistakably Tiger.
